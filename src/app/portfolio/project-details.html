Problem & Context

Workshop planning was largely manual and fragmented, resulting in:

  • Inefficient job allocation
  • Overloaded or underutilized mechanics
  • Limited visibility into daily and upcoming service schedules

Stakeholders needed a system that improved operational efficiency, while mechanics needed a clear, low-friction planning experience suitable for tablet usage on the workshop floor.

My Role

As the Senior UX/UI Designer, I was responsible for:

  • Requirement gathering and stakeholder workshops
  • Translating business needs into user-centric workflows
  • Conducting user research and usability testing
  • Designing wireframes, prototypes, and final UI
  • Leading multiple design iterations based on feedback

Discovery & Research

I conducted interviews with:

  • Workshop managers
  • Service advisors
  • Mechanics

Key insights:

  • Planning decisions are made quickly and often change during the day
  • Users needed at-a-glance visibility rather than detailed screens
  • Tablet interactions had to work in noisy, high-pressure environments

These insights directly shaped the interaction model and layout.

Problem Definition

How might we enable workshop managers to plan and adjust service schedules in real time, while giving mechanics clear, actionable task visibility with minimal interaction effort?

UX Strategy & Design Approach

  • Designed a time-based planning layout optimized for tablet use
  • Prioritized visual hierarchy over dense data
  • Reduced cognitive load by grouping tasks by mechanic and service stage
  • Introduced status-driven interactions instead of form-heavy inputs

Prototyping & Iteration

I created:

  • Low-fidelity wireframes to validate workflows
  • Interactive prototypes to test real-world scenarios
  • High-fidelity UI prototypes for stakeholder alignment

Several iterations were made to simplify drag-and-drop planning, reduce steps in task reassignment, and improve readability under workshop lighting conditions.

Usability Testing & Feedback

Usability testing revealed:

  • Confusion around job prioritization
  • Missed status updates during peak hours

Based on feedback:

  • Status indicators were redesigned for clarity
  • Touch targets were increased
  • Color usage was refined to improve quick recognition

UI Design & Visual System

  • High-contrast colors for workshop environments
  • Clear typography for quick scanning
  • Consistent component behavior across states
  • Scalable patterns for future feature expansion

The design system ensured consistency across planner views and related workshop tools.

Outcome & Learnings

  • Improved clarity and speed of daily planning
  • Reduced manual coordination between managers and mechanics
  • Strong stakeholder buy-in due to iterative validation
Key learning: Designing for operational environments requires prioritizing clarity, adaptability, and speed over visual complexity.

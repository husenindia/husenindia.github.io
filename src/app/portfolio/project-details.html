Smart Planner

UX & UI Flow Design Case Study

Overview

Smart Planner is an operational planning and service management application designed to streamline appointment booking, mechanic allocation, and service execution across dealerships and service centers.

The system supports real-time booking during live customer calls, automatic mechanic assignment, and end-to-end service tracking â€” from appointment creation to digital payment.

  • Role: Lead UX & UI Designer
  • Platform: Desktop, Tablet
  • Users: Call Center Executives, Mechanics, Operations Team
  • Ownership: UX Flow Design & UI Strategy

Problem

Booking service appointments during live customer calls was time-consuming and error-prone. Call center executives lacked a real-time view of mechanic availability, while mechanics had limited visibility into assigned work and service history.

The absence of a unified system resulted in overbooking, inefficient mechanic utilization, delayed service updates, and fragmented customer experiences.

Design Goal

Design a centralized planner that enables fast appointment booking, automatic mechanic allocation, and real-time service execution, while providing clear visibility to both call center staff and mechanics.

My Role

  • Defined end-to-end user flows for booking, planning, and service execution
  • Collaborated with stakeholders to align operational and UX requirements
  • Designed planner interactions and mechanic workflows
  • Created scalable UX patterns for internal tools
  • Validated flows through usability reviews and iterations

UX Strategy

  • Speed-first design: Optimized for live customer call scenarios
  • Calendar-driven planning: Day, week, and month visibility
  • Automation: Automatic mechanic assignment to reduce manual decisions
  • Role-based experiences: Distinct flows for call center and mechanics
  • Operational transparency: Real-time updates across the system

Key User Flow

  1. Receive customer call and initiate appointment booking
  2. Select dealership and service center
  3. View planner by day, week, or month
  4. Check mechanic-wise availability
  5. Identify the fastest available service slot
  6. Automatically assign the mechanic
  7. Create a work order linked to the assigned mechanic
  8. Mechanic views assigned job on tablet dashboard
  9. Mechanic uploads images, videos, damage reports, or adds services
  10. Track complete service history of the vehicle
  11. Complete service with digital payment

Mechanic Experience

Mechanics access a dedicated tablet dashboard where they can view assigned vehicles, understand service requirements, upload service media, report damages, and suggest additional services in real time.

This reduced dependency on verbal communication and improved accuracy in service documentation.

Outcome

Smart Planner enabled faster appointment booking during live calls, improved mechanic utilization, and created a seamless flow between planning, execution, and payment.

The system unified multiple operational touchpoints into a single, transparent experience for call center teams and mechanics.

Key Learnings

Designing internal operational tools requires prioritizing speed, clarity, and automation over visual complexity. Role-based workflows and real-time data visibility proved critical for reducing friction and improving service efficiency.

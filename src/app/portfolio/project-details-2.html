Contactless Car Service Application

UX & UI Case Study

Overview

A post-COVID mobile application designed to enable fully contactless car servicing. Customers can book, manage, and pay for services digitally, with support for both self-booking and assisted booking by service centers.

  • Role: Senior UX & UI Designer
  • Platform: Mobile Application
  • Industry: Automotive
  • Ownership: End-to-End UX & UI

Problem

Traditional car service processes relied on physical visits and manual coordination. Post-COVID, users needed a safe, transparent, and contactless way to service their vehicles.

My Role

  • Stakeholder requirement gathering
  • User research & journey mapping
  • UX strategy & interaction design
  • Prototyping & usability testing
  • UI design with multiple iterations

UX Strategy

  • Progressive disclosure to reduce cognitive load
  • Auto-fetch vehicle details using registration number
  • Step-by-step guided booking flow
  • Trust-driven design with cost transparency

Key User Flow

  1. Vehicle registration entry
  2. Service center selection
  3. Date, time & pickup/drop selection
  4. Service & parts selection
  5. Damage reporting
  6. Cost summary & payment

Outcome

The solution enabled a complete contactless service journey, improved user trust, and reduced dependency on physical interactions while maintaining operational efficiency.

Key Learnings

Designing for trust and clarity is critical in remote service experiences. Iterative usability testing significantly improved user confidence and task completion.

Contactless Digital Car Service Application

UX & UI Case Study

Overview

A post-COVID, end-to-end digital car service application designed for a UK-based automotive client. The product enables customers to book, manage, and complete their entire car service journey without physical human interaction, while maintaining transparency, trust, and flexibility.

The application supports both self-service customers and service call-center employees, and is designed to scale across mobile, tablet, and desktop platforms.

  • Role: Senior UX & UI Designer
  • Platform: Mobile, Tablet, Desktop
  • Industry: Automotive
  • Ownership: End-to-End UX & UI

Problem

Traditional car service journeys relied heavily on phone calls, physical visits, and manual coordination between customers and service centers. Post-COVID, these processes no longer met user expectations for safety, convenience, and transparency.

Customers lacked visibility into service progress, cost breakdowns, and vehicle handling, while service centers struggled to manage high call volumes and operational inefficiencies.

My Role

  • Collaborated closely with product owners and business stakeholders
  • Conducted user research with customers and service center staff
  • Defined end-to-end user journeys and system workflows
  • Designed interaction models and scalable UX patterns
  • Led prototyping, usability testing, and iterative UI refinement

UX Strategy

  • Contactless by default: No mandatory physical interaction at any stage
  • Progressive disclosure: Present information step-by-step to reduce cognitive load
  • Automation over manual input: Vehicle data auto-fetched using registration number
  • Transparency & trust: Continuous visibility of service status and cost
  • Flexibility: Support for pickup/drop, rental vehicles, and assisted bookings

Key User Flow

  1. Enter vehicle registration number to auto-fetch vehicle details
  2. Select nearest or preferred service station
  3. Choose required services with real-time cost visibility
  4. Select pickup/drop option or plan digital check-in
  5. Pick preferred date and time slot based on availability
  6. Opt for add-ons such as rental vehicles or service campaigns
  7. Track live service progress with photos and videos
  8. Complete digital payment and contactless check-out

Outcome

The final solution delivered a fully digital, contactless car service experience that empowered customers with control and transparency while reducing operational load on service centers.

The application improved user confidence, enabled real-time service tracking, and streamlined booking, servicing, and payment into a single cohesive journey.

Key Learnings

Designing contactless service experiences requires a strong focus on trust, clarity, and continuous feedback. Iterative usability testing proved critical in validating assumptions and refining complex service workflows into intuitive user experiences.
